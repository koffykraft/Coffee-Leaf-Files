Seasons & Phenology

The Rhythm of Growth · Timing · Cycle

Phenology is the study of periodic plant life cycle events and how they are influenced by seasons and environment. Understanding it determines when the best leaf is available.

Plant Care Index How to Read a Leaf
Citane Context — Thumpassery Estate

Kerala's two distinct monsoon seasons drive the flush rhythm at Thumpassery Estate. The southwest monsoon (June–September) and the northeast monsoon (October–December) create a year-round but seasonally variable pattern of growth and rest.

Nine Seasonal Stages
Rainy Season
Seasonal Stage

Moisture abundance triggers new leaf growth and canopy expansion. The primary flush season. Kerala's southwest monsoon (roughly June–September) is the main rainy season for Citane.

Dry Season
Seasonal Stage

Growth slows. Leaves mature and the plant concentrates compounds. Dry season leaves may carry a denser, more concentrated character than rainy season equivalents.

None →
Flowering
Plant Stage

Coffee flowers are brief, fragrant, and triggered by the transition from dry to wet conditions. Flowering synchrony depends on temperature and moisture. Energy is partially redirected from leaf production.

None →
Fruit Set
Plant Stage

Successful pollination leads to cherry development. Nutrition and water balance are critical. The plant's energy budget during this stage affects leaf production in parallel.

None →
Cherry Ripening
Plant Stage

Colour change signals maturation and sugar accumulation. High demand on plant resources during this period. Leaf quality during ripening reflects the energy competition.

None →
Post Harvest
Plant Stage

After cherry pick, the plant recovers and prepares for the next cycle. Pruning, nutrition, and rest during this window set the foundation for the following season.

See Pruning → Observed
Flush Cycle
Leaf Stage

New leaves emerge in waves, triggered by rain after a dry period. Each flush has a different character and harvest window. Age at harvest determines compound profile and processing suitability.

See Flush / Young Leaf → Research
Dormancy
Plant Stage

A period of reduced metabolic activity. The plant conserves energy and prepares for renewal. In Kerala's climate, dormancy is partial rather than complete.

Growth Cycle
Whole Year

The complete rhythm of growth, maturity, harvest, and renewal. A well-managed cycle produces consistent leaf quality across years.

Eight Factors That Influence Phenology
Temperature
Influencing Factor

Affects bud break, flowering, growth rate, and cherry ripening. Warmer temperatures accelerate all stages. Temperature variation between seasons is a primary phenological driver.

None →
Rainfall
Influencing Factor

Drives flushes, sustains soil moisture, and supports growth. Rain after a dry period is one of the most reliable triggers for new leaf flush.

None →
Sunlight
Influencing Factor

Influences photosynthesis, leaf thickness, colour, and compound levels. Day length also plays a role in triggering flowering and dormancy transitions.

None →
Humidity
Influencing Factor

Impacts disease pressure and water balance within the leaf. Kerala's high ambient humidity is characteristic of the Citane growing environment.

None →
Soil Health
Influencing Factor

Nutrition, organic matter, and microbial life support every stage of the phenological cycle. A depleted soil cannot sustain peak leaf production.

See Soil Health → Research
Altitude
Influencing Factor

Shapes temperature patterns, growth rate, and flavour potential across the full phenological cycle. Citane at 130 m operates in a warm, fast-moving phenological rhythm.

None →
Wind
Influencing Factor

Affects transpiration, pollination, and evaporation. Moderate air movement supports pollination and reduces disease pressure.

None →
Day Length
Influencing Factor

Photoperiod influences flowering, dormancy, and seasonal transitions. In near-equatorial Kerala, day length variation is moderate but still biologically significant.

None →

Key Principle

Observe. Understand. Respond. Work with the seasons, not against them. When we understand the seasons, we work with nature. When we honour the rhythm, the leaf expresses its true potential.
