Four Documented Buna Processing Traditions

Engere
Southern Ethiopia · Gofa Zone
  • Mature leaf selected
  • Minimal processing
  • Long boil in milk
  • Spice additions (tradition)
  • Served hot, household ritual

Documented: Yohannis et al. 2026, Awoke et al. 2026. Sweetness from extended milk boil.

Kuti
Harar · Eastern Ethiopia
  • Whole-plant knowledge
  • Extended water decoction
  • Multiple steepings
  • Amartasa, Sirriwabun (? unknown)
  • Social and ceremonial use

Documented: Wehib M. Ahmed 2015. Sweetness peaks with extended heat. Not a substitute for coffee — parallel tradition.

Kawa Daun
West Sumatra · Minangkabau
  • Robusta leaves used
  • Three processing methods
  • Smoked (wood smoke)
  • Flame-toasted
  • Slow kitchen fire

Documented: Novita et al. 2018. Active Minangkabau trading tradition. Smoke is a deliberate processing agent, not a defect.

Citane
Kerala · Thumpassery Estate
  • Arabica Chandragiri
  • Rubber shade, ~130m
  • Selective harvest
  • Controlled drying
  • Multiple brew formats

KoffyKraft adaptation. Drawing from documented traditions. Processing pathways in active development. Evidence-based approach.

Key Processing Terms

Withering

Controlled moisture reduction of freshly harvested leaf through shade spreading with airflow. Makes the leaf pliable for subsequent steps and initiates gentle enzymatic activity. Duration and humidity control are critical variables.

Bruising / Rolling

Physical breakdown of cell walls — by hand or machine — releasing cellular juices and initiating or extending oxidation. The degree of bruising is the primary control over oxidation intensity. A decisive processing choice.

Oxidation

Controlled exposure of broken cell contents to oxygen. Not spoilage — a deliberate flavour development process. Minimal oxidation preserves green freshness; extended oxidation builds darker, deeper notes. The degree defines the style.

Fermenting

Microbial transformation — bacteria, yeast, or fungi — that alters the leaf's chemical profile over time. Adds complexity, reduces certain bitter compounds, creates unique signatures. Requires temperature, time, and hygiene control.

Smoking

Exposing leaf to wood smoke during or after processing. Documented in Kawa Daun tradition. The wood type and exposure duration determine smoke character. Smoke is a flavour agent, not a preservation shortcut.

Sorting

Removing stems, impurities, broken material, and off-grade leaf to produce a clean, consistent batch. A quality gate. Hand sorting allows the most precise control. What passes through sorting is what goes into the cup.

🌿 Preserve Nature

Minimise intervention. Maximise what is natural. Every step should have a reason — and the reason should be the leaf.

📐 Precision & Balance

Every step is measured. Balance creates excellence. Precision means intentional, not rigid.

🔁 Consistency

Standards, skill, and attention in every batch. Built through documented practice, not intuition alone.

🤝 People & Tradition

Honouring knowledge, experience, and craftsmanship. Art and science are both needed. Both deserve respect.

📈 Continuous Improvement

Learn, refine, and evolve. Every batch offers data. Every season offers learning. Every cup offers feedback.
