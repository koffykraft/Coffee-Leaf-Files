A Living Language — From Leaf to Cup

Descriptors grounded in published research on coffee leaf. Evidence status marked on each term. Not borrowed from coffee or tea cupping culture — built for this leaf.

Flavour & Aroma
Top Notes
The primary scent and taste character. Most volatile — first perceived, often first to fade.
Green, grassy, and floral notes documented in Rigling et al. 2023 (ICC2023-14837). Honey-like (β-Ionone), floral (α-ionone, geraniol, nerol), citrus (decanal), green (hexanal) identified as key volatile compounds in coffee leaf.

Flavour & Aroma
Base Notes
Deeper, heavier compounds. Emerge with maturity of leaf, oxidation, or thermal processing.
Earthy and woody notes documented in mature leaf (Fibrianto et al. 2025 — higher TPC in older leaves). Mineral character observed in Fibrianto et al. 2025 (low-bitterness profile, closest to mineral water). Caramel, roasted notes from Maillard compounds in thermal processing.

3
Taste
The five basic tastes as experienced in the cup. Sweetness and bitterness are the primary axes for Buna.
Sweetness documented (hydroxyl compounds, sugars, amino-peptide complexes — Fibrianto 2025). Bitterness from TPC, caffeine (low in Citane). GABA enhances sweetness without bitterness. Extended wet heat increases sweetness, decreases bitterness — opposite to tea logic (Kuti tradition, Fibrianto 2025).

🍋 Sour Present, not dominant ✓ Documented

Texture &
Mouthfeel
The physical feel of Buna in the mouth — viscosity, weight, surface sensation.
Mouthfeel is a primary quality driver — ranked highest importance value (56.27%) by consumers in Fibrianto et al. 2021 study. Brewing method (infusion vs decoction) directly influences body and texture. Creamy character in milk decoction (Engere) is structurally distinct from water-based preparations.

5
Aftereffects
& Finish
The lasting impression after the last sip. Buna's finish is typically clean and low in bitterness.
Sweet return (hui gan) — polyphenol-receptor interaction converting astringency to sweetness. Documented in fine teas; observed in quality Buna. Clean finish associated with low TPC and low bitterness profile of well-processed Citane (Fibrianto et al. 2025 — mineral water bitterness scale comparison).

Overall Sensory
Impression
The holistic experience of the cup — mood, energy, and the emotional register of Buna.
Overall impression is shaped by caffeine level (lower than coffee), GABA content (calming), and compound profile. Buna is documented as holding promise as a functional beverage with complex sensory attributes (Fibrianto et al. 2021, 2025).

Citane Note

Citane is Arabica Chandragiri grown under rubber shade at ~130m, Thumpassery Estate, Kerala. Extended wet heat increases sweetness and decreases bitterness in this leaf — opposite to tea logic.
