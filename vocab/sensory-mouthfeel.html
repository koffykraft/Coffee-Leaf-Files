Mouthfeel, Finish & Impression

Texture · Aftereffects · Overall Sensory Character

Mouthfeel, aftereffects, finish, and overall sensory impression vocabulary for Buna. Evidence clearly marked on each term.

Citane Context — Thumpassery Estate

Mouthfeel and finish in Citane preparations reflect the TPC content, processing method, and brewing parameters. Oolong-style preparations are documented as high sweetness and low bitterness (Fibrianto et al. 2025). Decoction preparations follow different dynamics.

Mouthfeel Terms
Light
Mouthfeel

Thin, effortless body. Associated with lower TPC preparations and infusion methods. A valid and intentional sensory target — not a deficiency.

None →
Full-Bodied
Mouthfeel

Dense, heavy presence in the mouth. Associated with higher TPC and extended decoction. Higher phenolic content correlates with greater body in brewing studies (Fibrianto et al. 2024).

None →
Structured
Mouthfeel

A perceptible grip or framework in the cup, produced by tannin-protein interaction. Positive when balanced; excessive when astringency dominates.

None →
Creamy
Mouthfeel

Smooth, coating character. Associated with milk-integrated preparations (Engere). Not an intrinsic Buna character — produced by the addition of milk to the base brew.

None →
Silky
Mouthfeel — Observed

Smooth without friction. A positive mouthfeel descriptor sometimes used for low-astringency oolong-style preparations. Not directly documented in project research. Evidence: Observed.

None →
Round
Mouthfeel — Observed

Smooth, integrated, lacking sharp edges. Used for well-made oxidised preparations. Not directly documented in project research. Evidence: Observed.

None →
Brothy
Mouthfeel — Observed

Savoury, substantial. Sometimes used for extended decoction preparations. Not documented in project research. Evidence: Observed.

None →
Juicy
Mouthfeel — Observed

Bright, salivating. A positive sensory impression for fresh and fruity preparations. Not documented in project research. Evidence: Observed.

None →
Aftereffect & Finish Terms
Clean
Finish

No residual off-notes, no excessive astringency, no bitterness carry. Documented as achievable with optimised Buna preparations (Fibrianto et al. 2025). A positive result of good processing.

None →
Drying
Finish

Tannin-driven sensation that reduces saliva and leaves a dry coating on the palate. Produced by polyphenol-protein binding. Decreases with cold serving and optimised extraction (Fibrianto et al. 2024).

None →
Lingering Green
Finish — Observed

Persistent vegetal or grassy character in the aftertaste. Associated with non-fermented, minimal-processing preparations. Reasonable consequence of high hexanal content. Evidence: Observed.

None →
Sweet Return
Finish — Observed

A return of sweetness after the initial taste. Known in tea literature as hui gan. Associated with specific polyphenol-receptor interactions. Not documented specifically for Buna in project research. Evidence: Observed — do not present as established for Buna.

None →
Long Finish
Finish — Observed

A sensory impression that persists 30 seconds or more after swallowing. Not measured in project research for Buna. Evidence: Observed.

None →
Warming
Finish — Traditional

A warming sensation associated with spiced decoctions — particularly Engere preparations with added spices. Not an intrinsic Buna character; produced by spice additions.

None →
Refreshing
Finish — Observed

A clean, uplifting quality at the end of the palate experience. Sometimes described for cold-served preparations. Not documented in project research. Evidence: Observed.

None →
Cooling
Finish — Observed

A cooling or menthol-adjacent sensation. Not documented in coffee leaf research in this library. Evidence: Observed only if encountered in direct cup evaluation.

None →
Overall Sensory Impression
Complex
Overall Impression

Multi-layered, evolving character. A wide range of flavours documented across Buna processing styles (Rigling et al. 2023). Complexity is achievable and documented. Evidence: Research.

None →
Balanced
Overall Impression — Observed

No single note dominates; components are in proportion. A sensory quality judgement, not a measured finding. Evidence: Observed.

None →
Calming
Overall Impression — Observed

Associated with GABA content and low caffeine levels. GABA is documented in coffee leaf (Fibrianto et al. 2025). Whether drinking Buna produces a calming effect is a separate and more complex question — do not state as fact.

None →
Uplifting
Overall Impression — Observed

Gentle stimulation. Reasonable given caffeine content, but caffeine in Buna is documented as lower than in tea or coffee (Klingel et al. 2020). Evidence: Observed.

None →
Comforting
Overall Impression — Traditional

Warm, domestic, associated with household preparation in traditional contexts. A cultural impression, not a research finding. Evidence: Traditional.

None →
Grounding
Overall Impression — Observed

Earthy, centring. A sensory impression sometimes applied to decoction styles with earthy character. Not documented in research. Evidence: Observed.

None →

On evidence discipline in sensory vocabulary

Research-documented terms (honey-like, citrus-like, floral, green, fruity, sweetish, low-bitter) provide the framework. Observed terms add the practical language of cup evaluation. Hypothesis terms are possibilities worth testing. The distinction matters: what is documented constrains what can be claimed. What is observed constrains what can be asserted. Hypotheses should be stated as such.
