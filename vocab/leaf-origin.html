Leaf Origin

The Source of Character · Ten Growth Factors

Ten factors shape a leaf before it is ever picked. Leaf origin is not background — it is the first layer of quality.

Plant — The Source Index Plant Variety & Anatomy
Citane Context — Thumpassery Estate

Chandragiri Arabica at 130 m, rubber shade, Karavaloor, Kerala. Lowland altitude, high humidity, monsoon-driven rainfall, filtered light. These specific conditions define Citane leaf origin and distinguish it from highland or open-canopy equivalents.

Ten Factors — What Shapes the Leaf Before Harvest
Variety / Cultivar
Genetics

The genetic identity of the plant. Determines the baseline profile of compounds, aroma potential, and yield. Citane uses Arabica Chandragiri, selected for performance under Kerala's conditions.

See Plant Variety → Research
Altitude
Growing Environment

Elevation above sea level. Higher altitudes slow growth and concentrate compounds — documented to produce higher total phenolic content and more complex sensory profiles. Citane grows at ~130 m: a lowland leaf.

None →
Terroir
Place Identity

The combined expression of soil, minerals, topography, and microclimate. Terroir is why a leaf from Karavaloor differs from the same variety grown elsewhere. It has measurable chemical consequences.

None →
Soil
Growing Environment

Nutrition, pH, organic matter, and microbial life. Soil feeds the plant through the root system. Healthy soil produces healthy leaves; depleted soil produces compromised ones.

See Soil Health → Research
Rainfall
Climate

Amount and seasonal distribution. Rainfall affects growth rate, water content in the leaf, and compound formation. Kerala's monsoon seasons directly shape Citane leaf growth and harvest timing.

See Rainy Season → Research
Temperature
Climate

Influences metabolism, growth rate, and aromatic compound development. Warmer temperatures accelerate growth. Citane's lowland warmth shapes its particular aromatic register.

None →
Humidity
Climate

Affects water balance within the leaf, stomatal behaviour, and disease susceptibility. Kerala's high ambient humidity is a defining characteristic of the Citane growing environment.

See Disease Management → Research
Sunlight
Growing Environment

Light intensity drives photosynthesis and determines leaf thickness, colour, and compound levels. Under rubber shade, Citane receives filtered light — sufficient for growth, lower in bitterness than full-sun equivalents.

See Shade Leaf → Research
Wind
Climate

Strengthens leaf tissue, reduces pest pressure, and influences water evaporation. At Thumpassery Estate, wind is moderated by the rubber canopy — a further influence on leaf character.

None →
Annual Cycle
Time

The seasonal rhythm governing when flush occurs, when leaves mature, when harvest is optimal, and when the plant rests. Working with this rhythm produces better leaves. Working against it produces stress.

See Seasons & Phenology → Research

The Origin Chain

Origin → Potential → Care → Expressed in the Leaf → Experienced in the Cup. No step in processing can create quality that was not first established in origin. Great Citane begins long before processing. It begins where the leaf grows.
