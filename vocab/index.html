Buna Vocabulary

A Shared Language — From Leaf to Cup

A common reference for farmers, producers, processors, café staff, and researchers. Fourteen sections. Every term defined, sourced, and cross-linked.

Why this exists. Buna is a new category. Words borrowed from tea, coffee, or herbal medicine carry assumptions that do not always fit. This vocabulary builds a shared platform: one set of terms, agreed meanings, consistent language across the farm, the processing shed, the café, and the conversation. It is a living document — terms will be refined as understanding grows.

How to use this reference

  • Farmers and producers — Sections 02 through 08 for field and growing vocabulary.
  • Processing teams — Sections 09, 10, and 11 for process and thermal terminology.
  • Café and service staff — Sections 12 and 13 for sensory and flavour language.
  • All users — Section 07 (How to Read a Leaf) is the bridge between source and processing. Start here if you are new to Buna.
  • Evidence is marked on each term: Research (peer-reviewed), Traditional (documented practice), Observed (recorded in practice), or Hypothesis (reasoned but untested).
