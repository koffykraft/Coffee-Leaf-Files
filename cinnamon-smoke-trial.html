Cinnamon Wood Thermal Stabilization

Phase I: Raw Input

The Starting Material

The trial initializes with high-turgor, vibrant green foliage branches systematically stripped from naturally grown crops.

Both Coffea arabica and Coffea canephora (Robusta) leaves are tracked simultaneously to monitor specific species-level structural differences under uniform processing metrics.

Phase II: Thermal Vector

The Smolder Architecture

Discarding aggressive masking smoke, this run uses the slow peeling bark of an old, fallen true cinnamon tree (Cinnamomum verum).

The low-intensity hearth produces clean, gentle convection currents that act as a vector for subtle spice precursor integration, avoiding heavy or acrid ash markers in the final cup architecture.

Phase III: Distribution

Chamber Atmosphere

Leaves are organized uniformly inside a large, isolated induction chamber to ensure balanced air movement around individual leaf boundaries.

By trapping the light, ambient cinnamon warmth, the atmosphere remains consistently humid and gentle during the initial critical stage of cell moisture migration.

Phase IV: Micro-Climates

Managing Thermal Limits

Continuous validation via infrared mapping logs leaf surface limits across distinct sections of the mass.

A reading of 41.0°C confirms that the environment remains safely below the critical plant cell destruction point, supporting sustained metabolic processes during the initial 48-hour window.

Phase V: The Drying Taper

Tracking Convection Boundaries

As the trial enters Day 3, slight shifts in the convection boundaries show an upper surface equilibrium of 44.3°C.

This careful thermal ceiling guarantees that moisture depletion proceeds systematically from the inner leaf tissue outward, preventing early surface hardening.

Phase VI: Evolution

Day 3 Status

A macro perspective of the curing mass shows an completely even structural transition.

By avoiding rapid high-heat fixing, the foliage safely undergoes a prolonged enzyme and microbe driven transformation, smoothly changing color as chlorophyll systematically degrades.

Phase VII: Matrix Mapping

The Tissue Transformation

A detailed view of an isolated leaf confirms the accurate execution of the process framework.

The deep olive-tan color spectrum shows controlled oxidation, while the primary vein structures retain cell elasticity before breaking the final water activity ($a_w$) barrier.
