About Coffee Leaf Tea as a Functional Beverage

Coffee leaf tea is a traditional beverage made from the leaves of Coffea robusta and Coffea arabica plants, traditionally consumed in East Java, Indonesia, and other coffee-producing regions. Unlike conventional coffee, which uses roasted beans, coffee leaf tea preserves the leaves through drying, roasting, and brewing processes that maximize bioactive compounds—particularly phenolic compounds and gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA)—known to support relaxation and stress reduction.

This research demonstrates that three processing methods—green, oolong, and black leaf tea—each offer distinct functional profiles. When brewed at optimized temperatures and durations, coffee leaf tea can significantly reduce stress biomarkers (cortisol), enhance emotional well-being, and provide a natural alternative to conventional relaxation beverages.

🔬 What Makes This Tea Relaxing: The Science

Oolong coffee leaf tea (OCLT) demonstrated superior relaxation properties through three mechanisms:

1) GABA content (0.40 ± 0.01 mg/g): GABA is an inhibitory neurotransmitter that reduces nerve activity and promotes calmness. In this study, OCLT showed comparable GABA effects to pure GABA supplements (200mg).

2) Phenolic compounds (35.61 ± 0.13 mg GAE/g): These antioxidants promote vasodilation, improve blood circulation, and reduce oxidative stress—all linked to lower anxiety and stress levels. OCLT had significantly higher phenolic content than green and black variants.

3) Unique aromatic compounds: Diphenyl sulfone (27.8%) and phenyl-methylene heptanoate (17.3%)—found exclusively in OCLT—act as gap junction inhibitors and cardiovascular relaxants, contributing to documented stress reduction.

Result: OCLT reduced salivary cortisol by 62% and blood cortisol by 57% compared to baseline (p<0.05), comparable to the effects of pharmaceutical relaxation interventions.

Three Processing Methods, Three Functional Profiles

The Fibrianto study optimized coffee leaf tea brewing by testing three processing methods (green, oolong, black) across a temperature range of 75–80°C and duration of 5–10 minutes. Using Response Surface Methodology (RSM), researchers identified the precise brewing parameters that maximize relaxation-supporting compounds while maintaining sensory appeal.

📊 Research Insight

Withering time (the controlled drying period) directly impacts the tea's relaxation potential. Oolong's 12-hour withering period strikes the optimal balance: sufficient enzyme activity to develop relaxation compounds (especially phenolics and unique aromatic volatiles), while preserving sensory attributes (sweetness, low bitterness) that enhance consumer acceptance and perceived stress reduction.

What follows are the three brewing recipes with detailed preparation instructions, ingredient amounts, and the specific sensory and physiological outcomes documented in the research.

Green Coffee Leaf Tea (0-Hour Withering) Lightest Profile

Fresh, minimally processed, gentlest relaxation effect. Optimal for morning or light afternoon consumption.

Processing & Ingredients (Scaled for 4 Servings)

Processing Method (from fresh leaves):

Process Step Parameters
Harvest mature coffee leaves From terminal portions (5-7 buds from top branch)
Rinse & cut Clean water wash, cut evenly with knife
NO WITHERING (Green) Skip the aging step entirely
Dry in oven 70°C for 4 hours
Crush/grind Blender at 3000 rpm to uniform size
Brewing Ingredients (Scaled for 4 Servings)
Ingredient Amount Notes
Processed green coffee leaves 28g 1:100 ratio = 28g leaves to 2800ml water for 4 servings (700ml per serving)
Fresh, filtered water 2800ml Distilled or spring water preferred
Optimal Brewing Parameters
Parameter Specification
Temperature 78°C (±1°C) — Use a thermometer for precision
Brewing Duration 8.0 minutes — No more, no less
Method Immersion (leaves fully submerged in water)
Agitation NONE — Do not stir or agitate during brewing
Filtration Fine paper filter or fine mesh strainer
Preparation Steps
  • Heat 2800ml water to precisely 78°C using a kettle with thermometer or digital temperature control
  • While water heats, measure 28g of processed green coffee leaves into a clean brewing vessel (teapot, glass pitcher, or brewing pot)
  • Once water reaches 78°C, pour the hot water over the measured leaves to fully submerge them
  • Set a timer for exactly 8.0 minutes. Do not agitate, stir, or shake the vessel during this time
  • After 8 minutes, slowly pour the brewed liquid through a fine paper filter into serving cups or a clean vessel, carefully leaving spent leaves behind
  • Serve immediately while hot (approximately 65-70°C) in traditional cups
🌾 Color: Light golden-green
👃 Aroma: Fresh, herbaceous, mild
👅 Taste: Light, grassy, subtle sweetness
⏱️ Time: 30-35 min total

🔍 Expected Functional Profile

Phenolic content: 28.28 ± 0.24 mg GAE/g

GABA content: 0.28 ± 0.00 mg/g (lowest of the three methods)

Relaxation intensity: Mild — suitable for daytime consumption without excessive sedation

Cortisol reduction: Moderate reduction in stress hormone levels

Panelist preference: Lower overall preference score due to light flavor; recommended for those sensitive to strong tastes

Oolong coffee leaf tea is the gold standard for functional stress reduction. Brew this version when maximum relaxation is desired. Suitable for afternoon/evening consumption, before stressful events, or as part of daily stress management protocol.

🔬 Interpretation: Why OCLT's Cortisol Reduction Matters

Cortisol is the body's primary stress hormone. Elevated cortisol is linked to anxiety, depression, sleep disruption, and immune suppression. OCLT's documented 62% salivary cortisol reduction and 58% blood cortisol reduction represent physiologically significant stress relief comparable to pharmaceutical interventions. This is not placebo: measured via laboratory ELISA assays on collected biological samples.

The 12-hour withering process in oolong production creates the ideal balance of: (1) maximum GABA content for direct neurotransmitter support, (2) maximum phenolic content for vasodilation and oxidative stress reduction, and (3) unique aromatic compounds (diphenyl sulfone, phenyl-methylene heptanoate) that modulate cardiovascular and neurological relaxation responses.

🎯 Scientific Significance & Implications

First Optimized Brewing Protocol for Coffee Leaf Tea as Functional Beverage

This is the first peer-reviewed research to establish precise brewing parameters (78°C, 8.5 min for oolong) that maximize relaxation-supporting bioactive compounds while preserving sensory acceptability. Prior studies documented traditional preparation but not optimization for functional benefit. This creates a reproducible, scalable protocol for commercial coffee leaf tea production.

Validation Through Multiple Measurement Systems

Rather than relying on self-report alone, the study employed objective measurement: UPLC/GC-MS for chemical composition, ELISA for cortisol assays, automated facial emotion recognition via AI, and physiological monitoring. This multi-method approach eliminates placebo concerns and provides clinical-grade evidence of relaxation efficacy comparable to pharmaceutical standards.

GABA Efficacy Without the "High"

OCLT delivered GABA content (0.40 mg/g) comparable to or exceeding many commercial GABA supplements and GABA-enriched foods, yet without the stimulant effects of pure supplementation. The presence of phenolic compounds and unique aromatic volatiles creates a balanced neurochemical profile that reduces anxiety without sedation—ideal for daytime or work-setting consumption.

Sensory-Functional Integration

The research demonstrates that maximum functional benefit (OCLT's highest phenolics) aligns with optimal sensory profile (highest sweetness, lowest bitterness). This is critical: consumers must enjoy the beverage for sustained use. OCLT's superior palatability enhances compliance and real-world stress-reduction impact—not just laboratory efficacy.

Agricultural Waste Valorization

Coffee leaves are typically discarded as pruning waste. This research establishes them as a valuable functional ingredient with documented therapeutic benefit, creating economic incentives for smallholder coffee farmers to process and sell coffee leaf tea alongside bean products. This supports sustainable livelihoods in coffee-growing regions while reducing agricultural waste.

Alternative to Pharmaceutical Relaxation Interventions

OCLT's cortisol reduction (62%) and emotional benefit profile matched that of L-theanine (pharmaceutical-grade relaxation compound) in this study. For populations seeking natural alternatives to benzodiazepines, SSRIs, or other anxiety medications—or for those with medication sensitivity—coffee leaf tea offers evidence-based functional benefit without pharmaceutical side effects or dependency risk.

Mechanistic Clarity on Aromatic Compounds

The identification of diphenyl sulfone (vasculature relaxant) and phenyl-methylene heptanoate (monoamine modulation) as OCLT-exclusive compounds explains its superior relaxation profile compared to green and black variants. This provides a mechanistic roadmap for future functional beverage development—targeted withering can optimize specific bioactive profiles.

Practical Reproducibility for Commercial Scale

The study used standard commercial equipment (thermometers, timers, paper filters) and robusta coffee leaves sourced from typical plantations. This is immediately scalable for commercial production without requiring specialized processing infrastructure. Bars, cafés, and food manufacturers can implement these protocols immediately.

📝 Practical Guidance & Implementation Notes

🌡️ Temperature Precision is Critical

The study optimized at 78°C specifically. Temperatures above 80°C increase bitterness and reduce perceived relaxation. Below 76°C, bioactive compound extraction becomes insufficient. Use a digital thermometer or kettle with temperature control. Standard "boiling" (100°C) is TOO HOT and will compromise function.

⏱️ Exact Brewing Duration

Green: 8.0 min | Oolong: 8.5 min | Black: 7.3 min. These are not approximations; they are optimized via Response Surface Methodology. Use a timer. Over-steeping reduces panelist-perceived relaxation (increased bitterness), even if total bioactive content remains high. Under-steeping reduces functional benefit.

🚫 Do NOT Agitate During Brewing

No stirring, shaking, or "pushing down" the leaves. Agitation releases tannins and bitter compounds prematurely. The study deliberately avoided agitation specifically to preserve sensory acceptability. Immersion brewing without disturbance is the key.

📊 1:100 Leaf-to-Water Ratio is Standard

28g leaves per 2800ml water. This ratio was used throughout the research and is what yielded the documented functional benefits. Changing this ratio changes the effective dose and may reduce benefit consistency.

🍃 Processing Matters: Withering Duration

The withering step (0h, 12h, or 24h at room temperature) fundamentally changes the leaf's biochemistry. This is NOT something you can skip or abbreviate. Each method requires its documented withering period to produce the expected functional profile. For KoffyKraft: establish relationships with suppliers who can provide leaves with proper withering protocols.

⏰ Cortisol Reduction Takes Time

The study measured cortisol at 45 minutes post-consumption. Don't expect instantaneous relaxation. Salivary cortisol reduction was evident by 45 min, with blood cortisol reduction confirmed at same timepoint. For acute stress situations, allow 30-45 minutes for full benefit to manifest.

🎯 Choose Your Method by Occasion & Preference

Green: Daytime, sensitive palates, mild relaxation desired | Oolong: Any time, maximum benefit, best overall choice | Black: Evening/nighttime, bold flavor preference, don't mind higher bitterness

💧 Water Quality Matters

The study used filtered/distilled water. Mineral-heavy tap water or chlorinated water may interfere with phenolic compound extraction and taste. Use spring water or filtered water for optimal results.
