Processing Method × Flavour Profile
Sources: Steger et al. (2022) — El Salvador on-site production study. Fibrianto et al. (2025) — Green/Oolong/Black CLT optimisation. Traditional pathway documentation (Engere, Kuti, Kawa Daun).
MethodProcessingLeaf AgeFlavour ProfileKey Drivers
Japanese Green (JGTP) Steamed Young (1–3 months) Fresh, grassy, green, slightly fruity, mild bitterness Hexanal, (E,Z)-2,6-nonadienal, Mangiferin
Chinese Green (CGTP) Pan-fired Young / Mature Nutty, toasty, mild astringency Pyrazines, Maillard products
White Tea Style (WTP) Dried only Young / Mature Delicate, light, soft sweetness β-ionone, α-ionone
Oolong Style (OTP) Partial oxidation, 24h wither Young / Mature Balanced, low bitterness, sweetness, fruity-mild Limonene, Diphenyl sulfone, Phenyl-methylene heptanoate
Black Tea Style (BTP) Full oxidation Mature (3–6 months) Earthy, woody, higher bitterness Tannins, Guaiacol
Yeast Fermentation 12h, 25°C, Saccharomyces Mixed Floral, sweet, peach-like, complex α-ionone, Decanal, Ethyl esters
Wild Fermentation 12h, no starter Mixed Complex, variable, honey-like potential β-ionone, Lactobacillus esters
Kawa Daun Smoke / closed furnace Mixed Smoky, woody, aromatic, dark Guaiacol, Cassia volatiles
Kuti Flat-pan roast (Harar) Mature Dark, tarry, roasty, bold Pyrazines, Furfural
Engere Decoction + milk + salt Mixed Savoury-neutral, mineral, umami-adjacent, anomalous green persistence Amino acids, NaCl, Mineral salts
