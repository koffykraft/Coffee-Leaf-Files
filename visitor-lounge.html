Begin With A Cup

Coffee is one part of the plant. This library explores another.

Across coffee-growing regions, the leaves of the coffee plant have been boiled, dried, smoked, infused, mixed with milk, and prepared in household traditions.

Depending on leaf and process, coffee leaf can be green, floral, fruity, honeyed, roasted, smoky, or earthy.

Many of the traditions described here were observed long before the reasons behind them were understood.

Some of this knowledge is documented in field studies. Some is being explored through modern sensory and chemical research. Some is being tested carefully through Citane processing trials.

This site brings those strands together. Begin gently. Follow what interests you. There is depth when you want it.
