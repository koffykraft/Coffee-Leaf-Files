Citane is developed and documented by KoffyKraft at Thumpassery Estate.[span_156](start_span)[span_156](end_span) Coffee leaves have been consumed for centuries across Ethiopia (Engere, Kuti, Chemo) and Indonesia (Kawa Daun).[span_157](start_span)[span_157](end_span) Citane is not a replication of any tradition; it learns from all of them.[span_158](start_span)[span_158](end_span)

Part 2 — Understanding the Coffee Leaf

Leaf age is the single most powerful determinant of the chemical starting point. Phenolic concentrations drop as leaves mature.[span_159](start_span)[span_159](end_span)

Leaf Classification Portfolio

Young / Flush Leaf: Peak chlorogenic acid, peak mangiferin, high moisture (~73%). Highly reactive.[span_160](start_span)[span_160](end_span)

Mature Leaf: dark green, firm. Balanced parameters (~63% moisture). Main processing baseline.[span_161](start_span)[span_161](end_span)

Old Leaf: Large, low bitterness, ~74% drop in active chlorogenic levels. Soft, round extraction.[span_162](start_span)[span_162](end_span)

Senesced Leaf: Fallen or near-drop. Minimal active chemicals. Ground framework for classic Kuti paths.[span_163](start_span)[span_163](end_span)

Harvest Rejections

Reject insect-damaged, fungal-spotted, or yellowing diseased leaves.[span_164](start_span)[span_164](end_span) Intact cellular layout is non-negotiable for green and precise amber configurations.[span_165](start_span)[span_165](end_span)

Part 5 — Processing Pathways

Pathway 1: Green Citane (State α)

Destination: Fresh, green, light-bodied herbal profiles with maximum molecule preservation.[span_166](start_span)[span_166](end_span)

  • 1

    Harvest: Collect clean L1–L3 pairs and transport to shade within 2 hours.[span_167](start_span)[span_167](end_span)

  • 2

    Clean: Wash in ambient water and pat surface completely dry.[span_168](start_span)[span_168](end_span)

  • 3

    Heat Fix: Steam for 2–3 minutes at 100°C to destroy PPO loops immediately.[span_169](start_span)[span_169](end_span)

  • 4

    Dry: Bake in a circulating air oven at 70°C for 4 hours until crisp.[span_170](start_span)[span_170](end_span)

Pathway 3: Oolong-Style Citane (State β Light)

Destination: Smooth, tea-like, lightly amber liquor with delicate honeyed overtones.[span_171](start_span)[span_171](end_span)

  • 1

    Wither: Lay mature pairs in a thin layer for 12–18 hours at 20–26°C.[span_172](start_span)[span_172](end_span)

  • 2

    Roll Lightly: Hand roll for 3–5 minutes. Bruise margins without tearing cells apart.[span_173](start_span)[span_173](end_span)

  • 3

    Oxidise: Spread and rest for 2–4 hours. Halt when margins flash amber hues.[span_174](start_span)[span_174](end_span)

  • 4

    Fix & Dry: Pan-fire for 5–8 minutes or oven dry at 70°C to snap points.[span_175](start_span)[span_175](end_span)

Pathway 5: Roasted Citane (State δ)

Destination: Deep, toasted grain, caramel, coffee-adjacent profiles.[span_176](start_span)[span_176](end_span)

  • 1

    Base Check: Confirm dried base is below 8% moisture.[span_177](start_span)[span_177](end_span)

  • 2

    Roast: Apply dry pan heat (130–160°C) for 10–18 minutes, stirring continuously.[span_178](start_span)[span_178](end_span)

  • 3

    Cool: Spread instantly in a thin layout on a cold surface for 20 minutes.[span_179](start_span)[span_179](end_span)
