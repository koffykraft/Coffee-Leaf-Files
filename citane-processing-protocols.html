Overview: What is Citane Processing?

Citane processing is the systematic application of heat, fermentation, oxidation, and aging techniques to fresh coffee leaves—transforming them from perishable botanicals into shelf-stable, flavorful, functional beverages[span_7](start_span)[span_7](end_span). Each processing pathway produces distinct sensory and chemical outcomes[span_8](start_span)[span_8](end_span).

Citane is not a single method[span_9](start_span)[span_9](end_span). It is a framework encompassing multiple approaches, each offering different flavor profiles, functional properties, and consumer positioning[span_10](start_span)[span_10](end_span). The goal of this section is to map the complete landscape: what flavors are possible, how to achieve them, and what protocols ensure safety and reproducibility[span_11](start_span)[span_11](end_span).

Unlike coffee roasting—which is well-documented globally—coffee leaf processing is only recently being systematized through scientific study[span_12](start_span)[span_12](end_span). What follows combines peer-reviewed research protocols with documented traditional methods used in coffee-growing communities[span_13](start_span)[span_13](end_span).

Core Processing Pathways

Coffee leaf can be processed along multiple distinct pathways, each emphasizing different characteristics[span_14](start_span)[span_14](end_span):

Fresh & Light Pathways

Cold Fresh Citane: Enzyme-preserved, minimal oxidation[span_15](start_span)[span_15](end_span). Bright, clean, grassy[span_16](start_span)[span_16](end_span). Used fresh or briefly dried[span_17](start_span)[span_17](end_span).

White Citane: Slow shade-wilting with controlled finishing[span_18](start_span)[span_18](end_span). Honey-hay subtlety, premium positioning, maximum calmness compounds[span_19](start_span)[span_19](end_span).

Oxidative Pathways

Oolong Citane: Wilt + bruise + partial oxidation[span_20](start_span)[span_20](end_span). Layered complexity, floral-to-toasty progression[span_21](start_span)[span_21](end_span). Most versatile pathway[span_22](start_span)[span_22](end_span).

Roasted Citane: Post-dry roasting for deeper flavor[span_23](start_span)[span_23](end_span). Comfort, warmth, niacin enhancement, cereal body[span_24](start_span)[span_24](end_span).

Black-Style Citane: Heavy bruising, deep oxidation[span_25](start_span)[span_25](end_span). Malty, raisin-like richness, full body[span_26](start_span)[span_26](end_span).

Fermented Pathways

Lactic Fermented Citane: Tangy-sweet, gut-friendly functional appeal[span_27](start_span)[span_27](end_span). Bridges fresh and aged profiles[span_28](start_span)[span_28](end_span).

Koji-Assisted Citane: Umami depth, savory novel profile[span_29](start_span)[span_29](end_span). Specialized technique[span_30](start_span)[span_30](end_span).

Yeast-Fermented Citane: Fruity esters, craft-cider complexity[span_31](start_span)[span_31](end_span). Experimental[span_32](start_span)[span_32](end_span).

Advanced Pathways

Aged Citane (Puerh-Style): Compressed, aged over years[span_33](start_span)[span_33](end_span). Deep earthy notes, profound complexity[span_34](start_span)[span_34](end_span).

Smoked Citane: Light or heavy smoke exposure[span_35](start_span)[span_35](end_span). Pine-resin or char-caramel notes[span_36](start_span)[span_36](end_span).

Fire-Kissed Citane: Quick charcoal exposure[span_37](start_span)[span_37](end_span). Mild char, caramel, roasted depth[span_38](start_span)[span_38](end_span).

Each pathway can be customized further through leaf age selection, harvest timing, equipment choices, and finishing techniques[span_39](start_span)[span_39](end_span). Together, they form a ladder of expression: from core styles to premium reserves to experimental expressions[span_40](start_span)[span_40](end_span).

Citane Process Compass: Target Six Flavor Profiles

Instead of following a single pathway, Citane production can be goal-driven: choose your desired taste outcome, then follow the specific steps that produce it[span_41](start_span)[span_41](end_span). Each target flavor is driven by specific volatile compounds that can be systematically produced through controlled processing[span_42](start_span)[span_42](end_span).

Target 1: Honey-Like Sweetness

Driver: β-ionone (OAV 132–927)[span_43](start_span)[span_43](end_span)

Leaf choice: Older or yellow leaves (higher β-ionone potential)[span_44](start_span)[span_44](end_span)

Processing: Rolling or cutting before drying to enhance enzymatic release[span_45](start_span)[span_45](end_span)

Drying method: Sun drying (≥48 hours) or wild/yeast fermentation[span_46](start_span)[span_46](end_span)

Timeline: Wither 12h @ ~20°C → Ferment 12h @ ~25°C (yeast/Lactobacillus) → Sun dry ≥48h[span_47](start_span)[span_47](end_span)

Target markers: Honey-like aroma, measurable β-ionone impact >100 OAV[span_48](start_span)[span_48](end_span)

Target 2: Floral & Perfumed

Drivers: α-ionone (OAV 30–100), decanal (OAV 14–301)[span_49](start_span)[span_49](end_span)

Leaf choice: Young or mixed leaves (ionones linked to fresher tissues)[span_50](start_span)[span_50](end_span)

Processing: Rolling or freezing to rupture cells, then allow partial oxidation[span_51](start_span)[span_51](end_span)

Drying method: Sun drying or yeast fermentation to enhance floral volatiles[span_52](start_span)[span_52](end_span)

Timeline: Wither 12h → Ferment 12h → Sun dry ≥48h[span_53](start_span)[span_53](end_span)

Target markers: Perceptible floral bouquet, α-ionone and decanal among dominant volatiles[span_54](start_span)[span_54](end_span)

Target 3: Citrus-Like Freshness

Driver: Decanal (OAV up to 301), (E)-2-nonenal (OAV 1–11)[span_55](start_span)[span_55](end_span)

Leaf choice: Older leaves or roasted samples[span_56](start_span)[span_56](end_span)

Drying method: Oven drying (70°C for 4h) or roasting for more citrus-like aldehydes[span_57](start_span)[span_57](end_span)

Timeline: Wither 12h → Oven dry 4h at 70°C[span_58](start_span)[span_58](end_span)

Target markers: Crisp citrus aroma, decanal present at high OAV[span_59](start_span)[span_59](end_span)

Target 4: Fruity / Melon-Like

Drivers: Octanal (OAV 7–23), 2,4-nonadienal (OAV 2–18)[span_60](start_span)[span_60](end_span)

Processing: Rolling/cutting to stimulate enzymatic activity[span_61](start_span)[span_61](end_span)

Drying method: Sun drying for slow transformation + yeast fermentation (12h, 25°C)[span_62](start_span)[span_62](end_span)

Timeline: Wither 12h → Ferment 12h → Sun dry ≥48h[span_63](start_span)[span_63](end_span)

Target markers: Melon-like sweetness, octanal + 2,4-nonadienal perceptible in aroma profile[span_64](start_span)[span_64](end_span)

Target 5: Cucumber-Like Freshness

Driver: (E,Z)-2,6-nonadienal (OAV 18–256)[span_65](start_span)[span_65](end_span)

Leaf choice: Young leaves or shoots[span_66](start_span)[span_66](end_span)

Processing: Steaming (pot steaming until lid @ ~100°C) to preserve green freshness and deactivate enzymes[span_67](start_span)[span_67](end_span)

Drying method: Oven drying for clean profile[span_68](start_span)[span_68](end_span)

Timeline: Wither 12h → Steam briefly → Oven dry 4h at 70°C[span_69](start_span)[span_69](end_span)

Target markers: Clean cucumber aroma, strong (E,Z)-2,6-nonadienal expression[span_70](start_span)[span_70](end_span)

Target 6: Grassy / Green

Drivers: Hexanal (OAV 1–10), 4-heptenal (OAV 1–9)[span_71](start_span)[span_71](end_span)

Leaf choice: Young leaves with minimal processing[span_72](start_span)[span_72](end_span)

Processing: Blending or cutting to release grassy aldehydes[span_73](start_span)[span_73](end_span)

Drying method: Oven drying (fast, 70°C, 4h) locks in the green profile[span_74](start_span)[span_74](end_span)

Timeline: Wither 12h → Blend/cut → Oven dry 4h[span_75](start_span)[span_75](end_span)

Target markers: Fresh-cut grass aroma, hexanal clearly detectable[span_76](start_span)[span_76](end_span)

Standard Extraction Ratio

Standard brewing for all targets: 2g dried leaf in 200ml boiling water, infuse 5 minutes, remove leaves, cool briefly before serving[span_77](start_span)[span_77](end_span).

Complete Processing Protocols

The following five protocols are adapted from peer-reviewed research and documented traditional methods[span_78](start_span)[span_78](end_span). Each produces distinct flavor and functional outcomes[span_79](start_span)[span_79](end_span).

Protocol 1: Non-Fermented Control (Green/Vegetal Profile)

Purpose: Produce baseline green leaf character with minimal oxidation[span_80](start_span)[span_80](end_span). Reference style for comparison[span_81](start_span)[span_81](end_span).
Expected flavor: Green/vegetal notes, mild bitterness, grassy aroma[span_82](start_span)[span_82](end_span).

  • 1

    Harvest mature coffee leaves (3–6 months old)[span_83](start_span)[span_83](end_span).

  • 2

    Wash gently in clean water and surface-dry on cloth[span_84](start_span)[span_84](end_span).

  • 3

    Cut into 1–2 cm strips for even drying[span_85](start_span)[span_85](end_span).

  • 4

    Oven-dry at 60–70°C in ventilated oven (1–2 hours) until crisp[span_86](start_span)[span_86](end_span).

  • 5

    Cool completely and transfer to airtight container away from light[span_87](start_span)[span_87](end_span).

Brewing Parameters: 2g dried leaf in 200ml water @ 95°C; steep 7–8 minutes; strain[span_88](start_span)[span_88](end_span).

Protocol 2: Fermented (Fruity/Sweet/Floral Profile)

Purpose: Develop fruity, honey-like, and floral volatiles through controlled fermentation[span_89](start_span)[span_89](end_span).
Expected flavor: Honey-like, fruity, floral; softer green notes compared to non-fermented[span_90](start_span)[span_90](end_span).

  • 1

    Harvest mature coffee leaves (3–6 months old)[span_91](start_span)[span_91](end_span).

  • 2

    Wash gently in clean water and surface-dry[span_92](start_span)[span_92](end_span).

  • 3

    Bruise leaves by rolling or crushing to break cell walls (critical step)[span_93](start_span)[span_93](end_span).

  • 4

    Ferment in closed container with damp cloth at 25–30°C for 12–24 hours[span_94](start_span)[span_94](end_span). Leaves will darken and develop fruity aroma[span_95](start_span)[span_95](end_span).

  • 5

    Oven-dry at 60–70°C until crisp and stable[span_96](start_span)[span_96](end_span). Cool and store away from light[span_97](start_span)[span_97](end_span).

Brewing Parameters: 2g dried fermented leaf in 200ml water @ 95°C; steep 6–7 minutes; strain[span_98](start_span)[span_98](end_span).

Protocol 3: Mild Acidity (Bridging Style)

Purpose: Bridge gap between green/vegetal and fruity/fermented styles[span_99](start_span)[span_99](end_span). Creates balanced cup with gentle acidity[span_100](start_span)[span_100](end_span).
Expected flavor: Honey-like, fruity, with mild lactic or citrusy acidity[span_101](start_span)[span_101](end_span).

  • 1

    Harvest mature green coffee leaves, wash gently, and surface-dry[span_102](start_span)[span_102](end_span).

  • 2

    Pretreatment: Either wither in shade 6–12 hours until pliable, OR freeze overnight (−18°C) and thaw[span_103](start_span)[span_103](end_span).

  • 3

    Bruise leaves firmly by rolling or crushing to break down cell walls[span_104](start_span)[span_104](end_span).

  • 4

    Ferment in closed container at 25–30°C for 18–24 hours[span_105](start_span)[span_105](end_span). Optional: Add 1 tsp natural whey or sourdough water to support lactic acid bacteria[span_106](start_span)[span_106](end_span).

  • 5

    Oven-dry at 60–70°C until crisp, stable, and ready for airtight collection[span_107](start_span)[span_107](end_span).

Brewing Parameters: 2–3g dried leaf in 200ml hot water (95°C); steep 6–8 minutes; strain[span_108](start_span)[span_108](end_span).

Protocol 4: Mature Leaf — Withering Route

Purpose: Soften tough mature leaves and develop rounded flavor[span_109](start_span)[span_109](end_span). Withering allows gradual enzyme activity and sugar concentration[span_110](start_span)[span_110](end_span).
Expected flavor: Softer infusion, more rounded and full-bodied, with fruity/honey notes[span_111](start_span)[span_111](end_span).

  • 1

    Harvest mature green leaves, wash gently, and surface-dry[span_112](start_span)[span_112](end_span).

  • 2

    Wither in shaded, ventilated space for 6–12 hours until pliable[span_113](start_span)[span_113](end_span).

  • 3

    Roll or crush leaves firmly to break down cell walls[span_114](start_span)[span_114](end_span).

  • 4

    Ferment in a closed container at 25–30°C for 12–24 hours until fruity aromatic shifts occur[span_115](start_span)[span_115](end_span).

  • 5

    Oven-dry at 60–70°C until fully crisp and stable[span_116](start_span)[span_116](end_span).

Protocol 5: Mature Leaf — Freezing (Cryo-Treatment) Route

Purpose: Disrupt rigid cell walls through ice-crystal formation, then ferment to unlock sweetness and aroma[span_117](start_span)[span_117](end_span). Creates cleaner, brighter profile[span_118](start_span)[span_118](end_span).
Expected flavor: Cleaner fruity/floral notes with enhanced extractability[span_119](start_span)[span_119](end_span).

  • 1

    Harvest mature green leaves, wash cleanly, and surface-dry[span_120](start_span)[span_120](end_span).

  • 2

    Freeze in freezer (−18°C) overnight to disrupt cell walls through ice crystals[span_121](start_span)[span_121](end_span). Thaw at room temperature[span_122](start_span)[span_122](end_span).

  • 3

    Roll or crush leaves thoroughly after thawing[span_123](start_span)[span_123](end_span).

  • 4

    Ferment in closed container at 25–30°C for 12–24 hours to realize the full aromatic potential[span_124](start_span)[span_124](end_span).

  • 5

    Oven-dry at 60–70°C until crisp and stable[span_125](start_span)[span_125](end_span).

Sheng-Style Citane: Dry Aging Protocol

Sheng-style Citane is dry-aged coffee leaf, inspired by Pu'erh Sheng tea[span_126](start_span)[span_126](end_span). Leaves are made shelf-stable first (sanitized, dried to low moisture), then allowed to age naturally under controlled storage[span_127](start_span)[span_127](end_span). Changes occur through slow chemical reactions (oxidation, polymerization), not active fermentation[span_128](start_span)[span_128](end_span).

Result: A mellower, rounder, deeper Citane cup that evolves over months and years[span_129](start_span)[span_129](end_span). Reserve/premium product positioning[span_130](start_span)[span_130](end_span).

Materials Needed

Fresh coffee leaves, clean unscented cotton cloth, wooden planks or screw press, combi oven, weights/clamps, paper or cotton pouches, cotton bags for storage[span_131](start_span)[span_131](end_span). Environment targets: 20–25°C, 60–70% RH, odor-free[span_132](start_span)[span_132](end_span).

Step-by-Step Aging Sequence

  • 1

    Harvest & Sort: Select tender, healthy leaves[span_133](start_span)[span_133](end_span). Remove damaged or insect-bitten material[span_134](start_span)[span_134](end_span).

  • 2

    Wilt: Allow leaves to soften in shade or combi (low heat, gentle airflow) until pliable[span_135](start_span)[span_135](end_span).

  • 3

    Steam: Briefly steam leaves at ~100°C for 1–3 minutes to sanitize surfaces and soften tissue[span_136](start_span)[span_136](end_span).

  • 4

    Press: Wrap steamed leaves in cloth, sandwich between planks, and press down for 24–48 hours[span_137](start_span)[span_137](end_span).

  • 5

    Dry / Dehydrate: Transfer cakes to combi oven at 55–60°C with low humidity until they pass the strict "snap test[span_138](start_span)"[span_138](end_span).

  • 6

    Wrap & Age: Wrap cakes in paper or cotton pouches[span_139](start_span)[span_139](end_span). Store inside cotton bags at 20–25°C, 60–70% RH[span_140](start_span)[span_140](end_span). Monitor monthly[span_141](start_span)[span_141](end_span).

Aging Timeline

0–3 Months
Flavors settle, bitterness softens[span_142](start_span)[span_142](end_span). Immediate post-production freshness gives way to subtle mellowing[span_143](start_span)[span_143](end_span).
6 Months
First noticeable divergence from fresh Citane; smoother, deeper amber liquor[span_144](start_span)[span_144](end_span). Rounding becomes apparent in the cup[span_145](start_span)[span_145](end_span).
12 Months
Clear aged character evident[span_146](start_span)[span_146](end_span). Honey-woody aroma, mellow taste[span_147](start_span)[span_147](end_span). Approaching premium maturity[span_148](start_span)[span_148](end_span).
2–3 Years
Rich, earthy-mineral complexity[span_149](start_span)[span_149](end_span). Deeper body[span_150](start_span)[span_150](end_span). Aging effects become highly pronounced[span_151](start_span)[span_151](end_span).
5+ Years
Fully matured, smooth, profound but with loss of brightness[span_152](start_span)[span_152](end_span). Reserve expression with significant aging depth[span_153](start_span)[span_153](end_span).
Safety Notes

Cakes must be completely dry, crisp, and papery—not pliable[span_154](start_span)[span_154](end_span). Use breathable wraps (paper/cotton), never plastic[span_155](start_span)[span_155](end_span). Discard any cakes showing visible mold or musty odor[span_156](start_span)[span_156](end_span). Monitor storage parameters (20–25°C, 60–70% RH) regularly[span_157](start_span)[span_157](end_span).

Citane Expression: From Core to Reserve

The protocols and pathways outlined above form a ladder of expression for Citane[span_158](start_span)[span_158](end_span):

Core Styles: Non-fermented, fermented, and mild acidity protocols[span_159](start_span)[span_159](end_span). These are the foundational expressions—accessible, consistent, straightforward[span_160](start_span)[span_160](end_span).

Premium Expressions: Single-target flavor pathways (honey-sweet, floral, citrus-fresh, etc.)[span_161](start_span)[span_161](end_span). These require more technical control and ingredient specification but deliver distinct sensory experiences[span_162](start_span)[span_162](end_span).

Experimental Reserves: Sheng-style aged Citane, smoked variants, koji-fermented, yeast-fermented[span_163](start_span)[span_163](end_span). These push the boundaries of what Citane can be[span_164](start_span)[span_164](end_span). They require investment in aging infrastructure and specialized techniques[span_165](start_span)[span_165](end_span).
